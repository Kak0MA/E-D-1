In preparation:

In preparation for his project, we learned to draw in 3D using legos and 3d paper. This set us up to be able to sketch our house design. We also explored using circuits and microbit which taught us to code for light and movement for our house.

Overview of your project/process/Challenges, setbacks, and solutions:

Please break this down by roles and share the writing and documenting. Each individual will be graded on their role as well as their contribution to the whole project.

The builder worked with inspiration from the group to design a drawing of the house on 3d paper. The group members contributed to figure out measurements on all sides of the house. The designer then transferred the individual shapes of the house into illustrator. We had a minor setback because our original sketched measurements could not fit on our illustrator 16x24in artboard, so we compromised and divided our measurements by 2 which cleared up space. With this extra space, the Programmer suggested adding a spinning fountain to our design to add movement to the house. The builder sketched the fountain and the designer created the fountain shapes in illustrator. Our finalized illustrator design was sent to the laser cutter to be cut. A setback occurred once our pieces were cut. We realized that our measurements had been off and the pieces did not align. In addition, we forgot to include a hole between the pieces for the fountain in the illustrator design. We tried to assemble the house, but quickly realized it would be too time consuming to put it together when we knew we had to change our illustrator file for the gingerbread. We went back to the drawing board. Not only did we change the measurements, but we changed the overall design of our house. In our original design, we had two balconies from the second floor to the first. We decided to get rid of these as we didnt love the way that it looked and felt that it would be complicated given that we were starting to run behind on time. We changed the house to a simpler design with one larger house structure in the middle and two smaller house structures on each side. We added windows to our design, which was not included in our first prototype. Once the builder finished the 3d drawing (with correct measurements), the designer got to transferring the drawing to illustrator. While this was happening, the coder coded the interactions such as music, a servo, and lights with microsoft microbit. The illustrator file was sent to the lazer cutter. The cardboard pieces cut out and all the members of the group contributed to gluing together our second design prototype. We were pleased to find out that the pieces sligned and we were happy with our design. We agreed that our file was finally ready to be sent to the lazer cutter for the gingerbread. Once the gingerbread was cut we started to assemble the house. We struggled to put it together with frosting, so we resorted to hot glue. We began with the roof, adding blue sour strips to make roof shingles. Then, we started designing the front of the house. Our original idea was to pipe the icing down the house but the icing wasn’t thick enough so we decided to spread the icing

Programmer

As the programmer for this group, I have been responsible for ensuring that all of our interactions work seamlessly in the way we intend. I have been able to do that using Micro bit programming and have created some intricate music files while producing code that will serve a more functional role from rotation to lighting.

 

We aim to utilize multi-colored lighting around the perimeter of our gingerbread house as well as on the trees as extra decoration – really providing the Christmas feeling. This wasn’t difficult to implement as I just used RGB alternation and timing to correctly adjust the changeover for the lights.

 

The music required some skill and attention to detail. It was tedious to make sure that every note matched up with the correct beat. The code files for the music are long but we believe it will add more substance to our project. Additionally, we incorporated button interactions that allow a user to change songs on command. Two songs are available for listening.

 

Finally, we have our 180-degree rotation code, meant to be used with our Christmas tree and water fountain. These happen forever with no other interaction attached.  

 

Overall, the code creation has been enjoyable and I have learnt a lot about micro bit processing/coding. Extremely useful!
